# 📊 Analysis – Ask A Manager Salary Survey 2021

## 📌 Project Overview
This project presents an end-to-end data analysis and governance pipeline applied to the 2021 Ask A Manager Salary Survey dataset.  
The goal is to clean, validate, and analyze real-world salary data while applying data governance principles to ensure data quality, consistency, and reliability.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Apply data governance principles on real-world data  
- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Detect anomalies and inconsistencies  
- Conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
- Generate insights from salary trends  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas  
- NumPy  
- Matplotlib / Seaborn  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 🔍 Key Analysis & Insights

### 📊 Salary Distribution
![Salary Distribution](images/salary_distribution.png)

- The distribution is right-skewed, indicating the presence of high-income outliers  
- Mean salary is higher than median, confirming skewness  
- Most salaries fall between $50K–$100K  

---

### 📈 Salary vs Experience
![Salary vs Experience](images/salary_vs_experience.png)

- Salary increases significantly with experience  
- Growth is steep in early career stages and stabilizes later  
- Strong positive correlation between experience and income  

---

## 💡 Key Findings
- Most respondents earn between $50K–$100K  
- High-income outliers significantly affect the average salary  
- Experience is a major factor in salary growth  
- Salary growth slows down after mid-career stages
